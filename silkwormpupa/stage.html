Structure & Macronutrients

One pupa, two worlds. A rigid segmented shell encases a soft, fluid core — and behind that texture sits one of the densest macronutrient profiles in the animal kingdom.

Fig. 04 — Anatomy

The anatomy of textural duality.

Exterior: a rigid, segmented, spindle-shaped chitin exoskeleton (2–3 cm). Roasting or flash-frying delivers a satisfying structural crunch, and the color evolves from milky white to deep chestnut — the darker the hue, the richer the flavor.

Interior: driven by tissue liquefaction and cellular reorganization, the core is a soft, fluid matrix of concentrated fat and protein — akin to golden cheese meeting roasted nuts, melting on the tongue with profound umami.

Mouthfeel: a crisp, structured shell dissolving instantly into a rich, savory, melt-in-your-mouth core.

Fig. 05 — Dry Weight

An ultra-dense structural profile.

Per 100g dry weight, chitin is entirely concentrated in the structural exoskeleton, and the macronutrient ratio is extremely low in carbohydrates — heavily biased toward high-performance energy supply and tissue building.

Fig. 06 — Protein

The elite protein paradigm.

The amino acid spectrum is directly comparable to premium pork loin — the gold standard of traditional quality protein. And the pupa contains a full suite of essential amino acids in excellent proportions.

21.5g protein per 100g fresh weight — purpose-built for rapid human digestion and near-perfect utilization.

Fig. 07 — Lipids

Liquid gold & the 70.9% advantage.

Total fat lands at 13.0g per 100g fresh weight — and 70.9% of those lipids are functional unsaturated fatty acids, primarily α-linolenic acid, with no excess saturated fat.

Structure Is Function

Every layer serves a single mission.

From the chitin shell to the fluid core, from macronutrient density to fatty acid profile — every structural layer of the silkworm pupa exists to pack maximum nutritional energy into the smallest possible volume.
